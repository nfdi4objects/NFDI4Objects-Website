At the beginning of May, our co-applicant, the [Deutsches Bergbau-Museum Bochum](https://www.bergbaumuseum.de/en/), organised an internal hackathon on the topic of data integration, knowledge graphs and SPARQL endpoints with Torsten Schrade from the [NFDI4Culture](https://nfdi4culture.de/de/index.html) consortium. The focus was on the National Research Data Infrastructure, but also on the diverse data and the need for continuous interdisciplinary exchange with other institutions. During this hackathon, the first data from the Deutsches Bergbau-Museum Bochum, which originates from different contexts, was merged into a knowledge graph. A total of three of the humanities consortia are represented at the DBM: [NFDI4Objects](https://www.nfdi4objects.net/en/), NFDI4Culture and [NFDI4Memory](https://4memory.de/).
