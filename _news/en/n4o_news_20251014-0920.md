As part of the cluster meeting of the CC ‘[Authority Files and Community-driven Vocabularies](https://www.nfdi4objects.net/en/portal/ccs/cc3_authority_and_norm_data/)’ on **11 November 2025, 1:00 pm**, Marthe Küster will present the results of her master's thesis on "T*he World in Letters: Challenges in the indexing of historical geodata in digital editions*".

In the context of NFDI4Objects, historical geodata plays an important role in object indexing, as editions and archive materials represent potential sources with object information. In her master's thesis, Marthe Küster focussed on the letters to Goethe; the data will be successively published on the research platform ‘[PROPYLÄEN - Goethes Biographica](https://goethe-biographica.de/)’ until 2039. The data was provided by the [Goethe and Schiller Archive](https://www.klassik-stiftung.de/goethe-und-schiller-archiv/) of the [Klassik Stiftung Weimar](https://www.klassik-stiftung.de/goethe-und-schiller-archiv/) as a TEI-XML dataset with around 15,000 letters.

The project analysed the current status of the indexing of geographic information in the letter metadata as well as the potential challenges that arose when recording the letter metadata and enriching it with identifiers. These range from missing and vague location information to changing place names. The aim of the work was to show which challenges occur how frequently when capturing geodata in historical letters. Around 15,000 letter metadata were analysed using Jupyter notebooks (Python) and a sample of 300 letter texts were coded using MAXQDA.
