The last cluster meeting of the CC ‘Authority Files and Community-driven Vocabularies’ this year will take place on 9 December 2025 at 2 p.m. on the topic of "The standard data systems of [iDAI.world](http://idai.world/). Central building blocks for the development of the German Archaeological Institute's data holdings," presented by [Fabian Riebschläger](https://www.dainst.org/wer-wir-sind/mitarbeitende/noslug/603).

With iDAI.world, the German Archaeological Institute (DAI) operates a comprehensive, networked research environment consisting of specialised subject systems. It enables scientists at the DAI and partner institutions to document and publish project-related research data in a sustainable manner. At the same time, researchers outside the DAI can also publish related data supplements there after their contributions have been accepted in the DAI's journals and series. The data in [iDAI.world](http://idai.world/) is available to the archaeological and classical studies community worldwide for research and reuse.

The heterogeneous data sets are integrated via three central standard data and terminology services, each of which assigns uniquely referenceable identifiers: [iDAI.gazetteer](https://gazetteer.dainst.org/app/#!/home) for places, [iDAI.chronontology](https://chronontology.dainst.org/) for time concepts and [iDAI.thesauri](http://thesauri.dainst.org/de.html) for technical concepts. As the connecting backbone of [iDAI.world](http://idai.world/), they increase findability, interoperability and reusability across system boundaries.

The presentation will introduce the structure, roles and functioning of these three systems, highlight their strengths and current limitations, and outline planned further developments. The focus will be on concrete examples from [iDAI.world](http://idai.world/), experiences from operation, and the question of how standardised data and terminologies improve the quality and connectivity of archaeological information.

The meeting will take place via Zoom; registration is not required!
