A central element of our work is the creation of the NFDI4Objects commons. These are quality-assured and open-source publications from the CCs and TWGs. The results are created in a bottom-up process. This is why new contributions go through a public commenting phase, during which the entire community is invited to participate in constructive discussions and contribute to the high quality of our commons with their input! 

From the TWG ‘[Proof of Ownership in the Event of Loss: Use-Case for the Minimum Data Set](https://www.nfdi4objects.net/en/portal/twgs/besitznachweis-im-verlustfall-use-case-f%C3%BCr-den-minimaldatensatz/)’, **the contribution ‘Documentation standards and proof of ownership in collections - a comparative overview’ is now online for comment in our Community Hub**. By comparing existing documentation standards and case studies, the study determines which metadata uniquely identifies museum objects and formulates proposals for a possible adaptation of the recommendations for the minimal data set. 

The TWG is looking forward to active participation from the community. Interested parties can **[read and comment on the article directly here](https://community.nfdi4objects.net/assemblies/commons/f/1/proposals/13?locale=en)**. To use the comment function, a one-time [registration](https://community.nfdi4objects.net/assemblies/commons) with an e-mail address is required. The commenting phase runs for 4 weeks, i.e. up to and including Tuesday 16 September 2025.
