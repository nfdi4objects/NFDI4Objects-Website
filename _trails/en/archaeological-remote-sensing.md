## Project Description

The project "Archaeological Remote Sensing in the Roman Rhineland" aims to develop and describe generally applicable workflows and standards for the processing, evaluation, and long-term storage of remote sensing data, with a focus on Roman archaeological sites west of the Rhine.

## Duration

March 1, 2023 – June 30, 2025

## Goal

Establishment of unified geoinformation systems and standardized protocols for managing archaeological remote sensing and geophysical survey data using Roman Rhineland sites as a practical case study.

## Key Objectives

1. **Workflow Development**: Creating standardized workflows for processing remote sensing data
2. **Data Standards**: Establishing data standards for archaeological geophysical surveys
3. **Long-term Preservation**: Developing strategies for long-term storage and archiving
4. **Best Practices**: Documenting best practices for remote sensing in archaeology
5. **Interoperability**: Ensuring data interoperability across different platforms and tools

## Focus Areas

- Aerial photography and LiDAR data processing
- Geophysical survey data management
- Integration with GIS systems
- Roman archaeological site documentation
- Data publication and sharing protocols

## Task Area

This project is assigned to **Task Area 1: Documentation** and focuses on the documentation phase of the research data lifecycle.

## Participating Institutions

- German Archaeological Institute (DAI)
- University of Tübingen, Digital Humanities Center
- Helmholtz Center for Environmental Research
- University of Bamberg, Institute for Archaeology
- Regional heritage authorities in Rhineland-Palatinate
